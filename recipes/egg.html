Morning Eggs

Description

A classic, fluffy morning favorite that takes less than five minutes to make. Perfectly soft, buttery, and seasoned to taste, these scrambled eggs are the ultimate quick breakfast to kickstart your day.

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggs (fresh is best)
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter (for the pan)
  • 1 tablespoon milk or heavy cream (optional, for extra fluffiness)
  • A pinch of salf and black pepper (to taste)

Steps

  1. Whisk the eggs: Crack the eggs into a small bowl. Add the milk (if using) and a pinch of salt and pepper. Whisk vigorously with a fork until the yolks and whites are completely combined and slightly frothy.
  2. Heat the pan: Place a small non-stick skillet over medium-low heat and add the buttery. Let it melt until it's hot enough to coat the bottom of the pan, but don't let it brown.
  3. Pour and stir: Pour the whisked eggs into the pan. Let them sit untouched for about 10 to 15 seconds until the edges just start to set.
  4. Form the curds: Using a heat-resistant spatula, gently push the eggs from the edges toward the center. Continue gently sweeping the eggs across the pan, creating large, soft folds.
  5. Finish and serve: Remove the pan from the heat when the eggs are still slightly wet and glossy -- they will continue cooking on the hot plate. Plate immediately and enjoy hot!
